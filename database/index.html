Project Background

What are Haors?

Haors are bowl-shaped floodplain depressions located in the north-eastern region of Bangladesh, covering approximately 19,998 sq km and accommodating roughly 2 crore (20 million) people.

1

The Challenge

The haor region has long been lagging behind mainstream national development. People in this region are poorer than in other parts of the country, largely due to frequent pre-monsoon flash floods that damage boro crops - often the only crop cultivated in haor areas.

2

Government Initiative

The Government of Bangladesh (GoB) through the Bangladesh Haor and Wetland Development Board (BHWDB) formulated a comprehensive Master Plan in April 2012 to address flood management and holistic development in the haor area.

3

JICA Partnership

Japan International Cooperation Agency (JICA) conducted feasibility studies (2012-2014) and partnered with GoB to implement the Haor Flood Management and Livelihood Improvement Project (HFM&LIP) as a Yen Loan Project from 2014-2021.

4

Project Objective

To reduce flood damages, improve access to basic infrastructure, and increase agriculture and fishery productivity in the haor areas of the upper Meghna river basin, thereby improving living standards and economic activities.

Expected Outcomes:

Poverty reduction, job creation (especially for landless and women), protection of boro crops from flash floods, and improved rural infrastructure connectivity.

Three Project Components

The project comprises three interconnected components, implemented by different agencies to ensure comprehensive development:

Component 1: Pre-monsoon Flood Management

Implementing Agency: BWDB

  • Construction & re-sectioning of submergible embankments
  • Protection of boro crops from flash floods
  • Rehabilitation of flood management facilities
  • Water control structures and regulators

Component 2: Rural Infrastructure Development

Implementing Agency: LGED

  • Construction of rural roads and bridges
  • Culverts and boat landing ghats
  • Rural market development
  • Improved access to markets and services

Component 3: Livelihood Enhancement

Implementing Agencies: BWDB & LGED

  • 3-1: Agricultural promotion (BWDB)
  • 3-2: Fisheries promotion (LGED)
  • Improved farming technologies
  • Community-based water management
Beneficiaries:

The project targets approximately 3.8 million people (around 688,000 households) across 28 upazilas and 165 unions in the haor region.

EIA Objectives

The Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) for the Haor Flood Management Project aims to identify, predict, evaluate, and mitigate environmental impacts before project implementation.

Identify Potential Impacts

Systematically identify all potential environmental and social impacts during pre-construction, construction, and operation phases of the project.

Predict Impact Significance

Predict and evaluate the significance of anticipated impacts on air quality, water resources, biodiversity, land use, and local communities.

Develop Mitigation Measures

Propose effective mitigation measures to minimize adverse impacts and enhance positive environmental outcomes.

Environmental Management Plan

Develop a comprehensive Environmental Management Plan (EMP) including monitoring protocols and compliance mechanisms.

Stakeholder Engagement

Ensure meaningful public consultation and participation of affected communities, local authorities, and other stakeholders in the EIA process.

Regulatory Compliance

Ensure compliance with Bangladesh Environmental Conservation Act 1995, ECR 1997/2023, and JICA Environmental Guidelines.

Scope of Work

Baseline Data Collection

  • Physical: landforms, drainage, meteorology, air & water quality
  • Biological: biodiversity, habitat areas, ecosystem products
  • Social: demographics, land acquisition, displacement data

Impact Assessment

  • Air quality impact analysis
  • Water resources & hydrology
  • Terrestrial & aquatic ecology
  • Socio-economic impacts

Mitigation Planning

  • Avoid impacts through alternatives
  • Minimize unavoidable impacts
  • Compensate for residual impacts
  • Enhance positive impacts

Monitoring & Reporting

  • Environmental monitoring program
  • Compliance verification
  • Performance indicators
  • Periodic reporting to DoE

Study Area Coverage

The EIA study area covers approximately 2km radius around proposed infrastructure, adjusted during scoping to reflect local topography and land uses. This includes impact assessment on 14 new haors with comprehensive sampling for air, water, and soil quality.

EIA Guidelines & Regulations

National Legal Framework

1

Environmental Conservation Act (ECA), 1995

The main legislation for environment protection in Bangladesh. Section 12 mandates that no industrial unit or project shall be established without Environmental Clearance from the Director General, DoE.

2

Environmental Conservation Rules (ECR), 1997 & 2023

Provides detailed procedures for EIA, project categorization (Green, Yellow, Orange, Red), and Environmental Clearance Certificate (ECC) requirements.

3

National Environmental Policy, 1992 (Revised 2018)

Developed in response to Rio Declaration, establishing policy objectives for environmental conservation and sustainable development.

4

EIA Guidelines for Industries (DoE)

Provides six-step EIA process: Screening, Scoping, Baseline Data Generation, Impact Assessment, Mitigation of Impacts, and Environmental Management Plan.

Donor Agency Guidelines

JICA Environmental Guidelines

  • Category A projects require full EIA
  • Land acquisition & compensation requirements
  • Public consultation mandates
  • Gap analysis with national regulations

World Bank Safeguard Policies

  • OP 4.01 Environmental Assessment
  • OP 4.04 Natural Habitats
  • OP 4.11 Physical Cultural Resources
  • OP 4.37 Safety of Dams

International Treaties & Conventions

Bangladesh is a signatory to several international environmental conventions relevant to wetland management and biodiversity conservation:

Ramsar Convention

1971

Convention on Wetlands of International Importance. Bangladesh has designated several Ramsar sites including Tanguar Haor (designated 2000) and Hakaluki Haor.

Convention on Biological Diversity (CBD)

1992

Focuses on conservation of biological diversity, sustainable use of components, and fair sharing of genetic resources benefits.

CITES

1973

Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species. Protects wildlife through international trade regulations. Bangladesh joined in 1981.

UNFCCC

1992

United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change. Bangladesh follows Common but Differentiated Responsibilities (CBDR) principle.

CMS (Bonn Convention)

1979

Convention on Migratory Species. Protects migratory birds visiting haor wetlands including thousands of waterfowl species.

World Heritage Convention

1972

UNESCO convention for protection of cultural and natural heritage. The Sundarbans is a designated World Heritage Site.

Tanguar Haor - Ramsar Site:

Tanguar Haor in Sunamganj district was declared an Ecologically Critical Area (ECA) in 1999 and a Ramsar site in 2000, highlighting the international significance of haor wetlands.

Project Categories (ECR Classification)

Under Bangladesh's Environmental Conservation Rules (ECR), projects are classified into four categories based on their potential environmental impact:

GREEN Negligible/Positive Impact

No EIA required. Example: Plantations, nurseries

ORANGE-A Minor/Temporary Impact

IEE required. Example: Tube wells, small ponds

ORANGE-B Moderate Impact

IEE + Site Clearance required. Example: Solar plants >50MW

RED Significant Impact

Full EIA + IEE required. Example: Large infrastructure, dredging

Haor Project Classification:

The Haor Flood Management Project falls under RED Category due to activities like river protection works, dredging/excavation, and embankment construction. This requires both IEE for site clearance and full EIA for Environmental Clearance Certificate (ECC).

Screening the Project & Why

What is Screening?

Screening is the first stage of environmental assessment that determines whether a project requires EIA and at what level of detail. It helps avoid wasting time and resources on projects with insignificant environmental impacts.

1

Project Classification

The project is classified based on Schedule 1 of ECR, which lists project types under Green, Orange-A, Orange-B, and Red categories.

2

Site Suitability Assessment

Evaluation of project location considering proximity to sensitive areas like Ecologically Critical Areas (ECAs), protected areas, and human settlements.

3

Impact Significance Check

Preliminary assessment of potential impacts on air, water, land, biodiversity, and socio-economic conditions.

4

Decision & Documentation

Based on screening, determine if IEE or full EIA is needed, and proceed with appropriate documentation.

Why This Project Requires Full EIA

Construction Activities

  • River protection works
  • Dredging and excavation
  • Embankment construction
  • Large-scale infrastructure

Sensitive Ecosystems

  • Proximity to Ramsar sites
  • Ecologically Critical Areas
  • Migratory bird habitats
  • Fish breeding grounds

Social Considerations

  • Large affected population
  • Land acquisition needs
  • Livelihood impacts
  • Displacement concerns
JICA Category A Classification:

Under JICA Environmental Guidelines, the project is classified as Category "A" requiring full EIA due to potentially significant environmental impacts. This aligns with the "Red" category under Bangladesh ECR.
